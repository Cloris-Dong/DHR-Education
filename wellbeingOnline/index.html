Well-being Online

This topic focuses on how to use the digital world in a healthy manner.
Identified sub-topics include:
  1. Empathy and Etiquette
  2. Awareness of Actions

I. Empathy and Etiquette

As the digital world is a public space, users are responsible for how their behavior online affects others.
Through education, users may develop the tools required for healthy digital interactions.

Examples of digital literacy regarding well-being include:
  1. Recognizing and understanding the feelings and perspectives of others
  2. How to set boundaries online and share personal information
  3. Identifying safe spaces in the digital world
  4. Knowing how to identify users who have the intention of harming others and respond accordingly

As everyone has a right to participate and develop a relationship with the Digital World, this in turn means users are responsible for their actions in this public space, see "VI. Rights vs. Responsibilities" on the home page
